Projects

Virtual Reality Digital Twin for Troubleshooting Lunar-based Assembly Failures (2020-2022)

NASA will return to the Moon to conduct lunar and space science as well as prepare for human exploration missions. NASA's current rover failure response methods use hardware duplicates which are costly and time consuming to assemble. To address this, we developed and assessed a VR digital rover twin in a simulated environment to support lunar robotic missions missions like FARSIDE in case of assembly failures.

  • Developed a rover digital twin in VR with a simulated lunar environment.
  • Replicated of rover and its environment in VR with identical control interface.
  • Used novel technologies (VR headset, stereo cameras) to assist teleoperators in troubleshooting.
  • Compared our design with physical hardware duplicates traditionally used for recovering from remote robotic operative failures where human operators cannot be placed locally with the rover.

Work supported by NASA-funded NESS and NASA Solar System Exploration Research Virtual Institute.
